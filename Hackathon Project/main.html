Welcome to Okinawa

Points of Interest

1: Naha International Airport

Naha Airport (那覇空港, Naha Kūkō) is a second class airport located 4 km (2.5 mi) west of the city hall in Naha, Okinawa. It is Japan's seventh busiest airport and the primary air terminal for passengers and cargo traveling to and from Okinawa Prefecture, Japan.

2: American Village

If you're in the mood for some Americana, head to Mihama American Village, a large entertainment and shopping complex in Chatan. As you wander through the mall, located under an hour from Naha, you'll experience a classic taste of America mixed with local Okinawan flavor.

3: Churaumi Aquarium

This aquarium is one of the world's largest and features sharks, coral, fish, manta rays & more.

It is an absolute must see while visiting the island!

4: Nago Pineapple Park

They have everything from pineapple wine to cookies and even cakes!

A small, enjoyable theme park about pineapples.

5: Cape Maeda

Cape Maeda is known for its coral plateau located on the west coast of central Okinawa. Huge rocks lie among the tip of cape formed by the raised coral reef, and right underneath you will be able to see one of Okinawa’s clear emerald green ocean with its beautiful coral reef.

6: Shurijo Castle Park

Shuri Castle was a Ryukyuan gusuku castle in Shuri, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan. Between 1429 and 1879, it was the palace of the Ryukyu Kingdom, before becoming largely neglected. In 1945, during the Battle of Okinawa, it was almost completely destroyed. After the war, the castle was re-purposed as a university campus.

7: Peace Memorial Park

Okinawa Senseki Quasi-National Park is a Quasi-National Park around the battlefields of south Okinawa, Japan. It was established as a Prefectural Park in 1965 and redesignated with the return of Okinawa to Japanese administration in 1972.

8: Gyokusendo Cave

Okinawa has over 600 limestone caverns, and among them, Okinawa World's Gyokusendo is the largest. The cave stretches for over 5,000 meters, and contains more than one million stalactites.

9: Katsuren Castle Ruins

Katsuren Castle (勝連城, Katsuren jō) is a Ryukyuan gusuku in Uruma, Okinawa. In 2000, Katsuren Castle was designated as a World Heritage Site, as a part of the Gusuku Sites and Related Properties of the Kingdom of Ryukyu.
